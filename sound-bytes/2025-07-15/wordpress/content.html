Today is National Leiomyosarcoma Awareness Day

Together in Strength, United for a Cure – Honoring Lives and Advancing Hope for Leiomyosarcoma

First launched in 2016 through Congressional recognition, National Leiomyosarcoma Awareness Day continues each year through our unwavering dedication — even though it is not formally acknowledged annually by Congress. Each year, we faithfully submit our Resolution through the Rare Cancer Caucus, holding onto hope that it will once again receive Congressional recognition.

But the truth is this: for those affected by this rare cancer, EVERY DAY is Awareness Day. We raise awareness, educate our communities, and honor the strength of survivors while championing critical research.

Lift the Fog of “Chemo Brain”

  • Get Moving: Regular physical activity boosts brain function and reduces inflammation.
  • Try Mindfulness: Meditation, yoga, and tai chi help calm the mind and support memory.
  • Eat Brain-Friendly Foods: Whole grains, fruits, veggies, and omega-3s nourish your brain.
  • Prioritize Sleep: Stick to a routine and create a restful environment.
  • Use Tools & Tricks: Planners, reminders, and cognitive rehab strategies can help.

Financial Support for Cancer Survivors

A cancer diagnosis often brings significant financial strain — for treatment, bills, and everyday expenses. The Cancer Journeys Foundation highlights key resources to help ease this burden.

  • Federal Assistance: Learn about deferment options and protections during treatment.
  • Support for Students: Scholarships and grants for young survivors and families.
  • Transportation Help: Non‑emergency travel assistance for appointments.
  • Other Tools & Organizations: Foundations, legal services, and nonprofits offering grants and personalized support.
